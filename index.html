Japanese Koi

Most Kois outlive their owners and can have a lifespan of 200+ years in optimal conditions! That is due to the fact that the hibernate in the winter.

Scientific name: Cyprinus rubrofuscus
Average Length: 60 centimetres
Average lifespan: 40 years
Habitat: mostly fishponds, but they can also survive in wild freshater bodies such as ponds and lakes

The Japanese Koi is a domesticated version of a common carp. They are distinguished by their coloration, patterning and scaling. In Japanese the word koi is a homphone for affection and love, that is why it symbolizes love and friendship.
